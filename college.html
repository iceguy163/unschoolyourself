College is a popular choice for teens and young adults after they finish school. Since you are reading this, probably either want to go to college or are thinking about it.

But if you are unschooled, you may believe that your nonconventional education would close the doors to college.

But this of course is not true.

If you are a current or aspiring unschooler, and you want to go to college, that is absolutely possible.

Here are my tips:

Get a GED/HiSET

Many colleges and universities require one to have a high school diploma or equivalent. If it is needed to get into your desired college, consider studying for and taking the GED or HiSET test. The higher the score, the more likely you will be to be accepted into the college. You could even get college credit!

Take the SAT or ACT test

Other common tests taken to enter college are the SAT and ACT tests. However, while the GED and HiSET are taken to get a high school equivalency, the SAT and ACT are mainly taken as a standardized test in high schools and may be required to attend certain colleges and universities.

By studying for the SAT or ACT test and passing, your score may give you a better chance at getting into your desired college.

Build a portfolio

To stand out from all the other applicants, try creating a portfolio of projects, skills, etc. that you made/have. These could be art projects you made, a photo album you took, small little games you coded, etc.

Consider attending community college

Community colleges are 2-year long institutions that are generally cheaper than 4-year colleges. Most community colleges will accept anyone with a high school diploma or equivalent. If you attend community college and do well in it, you may have a greater chance into getting into a 4-year college, and save some tuition money too!

Conclusion

College is a great and prestigious choice for those who choose to go there.

Your nonconventional learning, as I have shown, will not mean you can't go to college.

In fact, being a self-directed and self-motivated learner could make colleges more likely accept to you. By showing that you are willing to learn things, that you can commit to goals, and are self-motivated, the doors to college will be wide open.
